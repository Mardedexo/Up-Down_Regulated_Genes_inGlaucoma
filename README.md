# Up-Down_Regulated_Genes_inGlaucoma
This project analyzes the top ten up- and down-regulated genes in glaucoma.

### Abstract

Glaucoma is the leading cause of irreversible blindness worldwide, with a multifactorial aetiology that includes genetic components not yet fully understood. This study aimed to explore differentially expressed genes (DEGs) in glaucoma compared to a group with no eye pathology. Two datasets containing transcriptomics data from the trabecular meshwork and astrocytes of the optic nerve head were selected from the public database Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) and analysed using the in-built limma package of R. The Benjamini and Hochberg false discovery rate was applied to determine significance, with a p-value ≤ 0.05 considered significant. Genes with a log two-fold change ≥ 1 were recognised as upregulated, while those ≤ -1 were considered downregulated in the glaucoma cohort. The ten most upregulated and downregulated DEGs were selected for further analysis. A protein-to-protein interaction network with standard settings was generated using the Search Tool for the Retrieval of Interacting Genes/Proteins (STRING). Functional annotation for Gene Ontology and pathway enrichment analysis was performed using the Database for Annotation, Visualization and Integrated Discovery (DAVID) with default parameters. Upregulated DEGs in the trabecular meshwork were enriched in haemoglobin-related processes, while downregulated were in structural and extracellular matrix-associated mechanisms, and in immunologic responses. The enrichment of DEGs in astrocytes of the optic nerve head was associated with metabolic processes. Although, any of the DEGs may be further explored as therapeutic target, AKR1C3, CALML3, and ENO2 should be prioritised. Additionally, ENO2 also showed promise for diagnostic applications.
